    • 2. IMPLIED DETAIL QUESTON

    (n) Implied detail question is a type of question that requires the reader or listener to infer or deduce information that is not explicitly
    stated in the given text or conversation. These questions typically rely on clues, context, and the reader's background knowledge to answer them.
    For example, let's say you read a passage that describes a character arriving home, but it doesn't mention anything about the character's mood.
    An implied detail question could be:

    "Based on the character's actions and the description of the setting, how would you describe the character's mood
    when they arrived home?"

    In this case, you have to analyze the information provided and make an inference about the character's mood, even though it is not explicitly stated.
    Implied detail questions require critical thinking skills and the ability to read between the lines to understand the deeper meaning or implications in
    a text or conversation.

    2. TRANSITION QUESTION

    (n) Transition question is a type of question that help smoothly move the conversation from one topic ore point to another.
    It is used to transition form one idea, subject, or phase of discussion to the next.
    For example:
    "I think it's worth exploring another angle related to (current topic)
    What do you think about?" (new angle or subtopic)
    "That's an interesting point you made about (current topic)
    Now, shifting gears a bit, i'd like to discuss, what are you thoughts on that?" (new topic)

    Transition question can very depending on the context and the specific purpose of the conversation.
    As we know, the goal is to smoothly transition from one topic to another while keeping the
    conversation engaging and coherent.
